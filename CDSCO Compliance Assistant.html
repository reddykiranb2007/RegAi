Classification Guide

Class A - Low Risk

Risk Level: Minimal potential harm to user

Regulatory Authority: State Licensing Authority (SLA)

Medical Devices Examples:

  • Elastic bandages, examination gloves
  • Thermometers (non-electronic)
  • Absorbent cotton, surgical instruments
  • Hot water bags, sphygmomanometer

IVD Examples:

  • Laboratory reagents and solutions
  • Non-specific laboratory testing materials

Requirements:

  • Registration (for non-sterile, non-measuring devices)
  • MD5 License (for sterile or measuring devices)
  • Quality Management System (QMS) per ISO 13485:2016
  • Device Master File (DMF)
  • Exemption from clinical evaluation for most

Class B - Low Moderate Risk

Risk Level: Moderate potential harm; moderate controls needed

Regulatory Authority: State Licensing Authority (SLA)

Medical Devices Examples:

  • Blood pressure monitoring devices
  • Hypodermic needles, suction equipment
  • Nebulizers, wheelchairs, catheters
  • Extremity MRI systems

IVD Examples:

  • Blood glucose test strips
  • Pregnancy test kits
  • Urine analysis test strips
  • Self-testing devices (preliminary results)

Requirements:

  • MD5 Manufacturing License or Import License
  • Quality Management System (ISO 13485:2016)
  • Risk analysis and risk management
  • Technical documentation
  • Clinical evaluation may be required
  • State-level inspection by notified body

Class C - Moderate-High Risk

Risk Level: Significant potential harm; strict controls required

Regulatory Authority: Central Licensing Authority (CDSCO)

Medical Devices Examples:

  • Infusion pumps, X-ray machines
  • MRI machines, CT scanners
  • Bone plates, powered wheelchairs
  • Cardiovascular imaging systems

IVD Examples:

  • Hepatitis testing kits
  • HIV testing kits
  • Cancer diagnostic kits
  • Coagulation parameter test kits

Requirements:

  • MD5 or MD9 License from CDSCO
  • Quality Management System (ISO 13485:2016)
  • Clinical Evaluation Report (CER)
  • Performance Evaluation Report (for IVDs)
  • Comprehensive risk analysis and management
  • CDSCO technical review and approval
  • CDSCO inspection required

Class D - High Risk

Risk Level: Highest potential harm; strictest controls

Regulatory Authority: Central Licensing Authority (CDSCO)

Medical Devices Examples:

  • Heart valves, implantable pacemakers
  • Implantable defibrillators
  • Artificial joints, intravascular devices
  • Active implantable medical devices

IVD Examples:

  • Companion diagnostics
  • Genetic testing kits
  • Blood compatibility testing for transplantation
  • Transmissible agent detection (organ transplant)

Requirements:

  • MD5 or MD9 License from CDSCO only
  • Quality Management System (ISO 13485:2016)
  • Comprehensive Clinical Evaluation Report (CER)
  • Clinical Investigation data in many cases
  • Detailed risk analysis and management
  • Biocompatibility testing (if applicable)
  • Rigorous CDSCO technical review
  • CDSCO inspection and site audit
  • Mandatory post-market surveillance plan
